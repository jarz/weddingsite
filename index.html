About Jenna & Tim

This love story begins like all other great love stories before it... on the internet. Thanks to the power of the world wide web, comparably nerdy individuals both originally from Northwest Indiana found each other in Indianapolis.

The internet convinced our main characters they had enough in common to motivate a relatively awkward first date at a subpar restaurant. Thankfully the awkwardness of the first date was not enough to discourage a second date. For her, it was because he donated his old car to the local NPR station, for him, it was because she had traveled around the world. The second date was much less awkward and thankfully lead to many more dates and a successful birthday trip to Nashville. So successful in fact, that soon after that our main characters decided to live together, combine their households, and start a zoo comprised of two cats and a dog.

Since moving in together, they have traveled to Colorado, the Pacific Northwest, Ireland and Scotland. They have also visited delicious restaurants, explored museums and galleries, saw amazing concerts (one so great they got engaged during it), fell more in love with Indianapolis and each other. They love each other so much, that they want to celebrate their love with you. Jenna and Tim hope their nuptials give all their guests a glimpse into both their love for each other, as well as the city that they love.

Staying in Indy

Our guest list is pretty small and the room blocks we looked into only offered a discount of a few dollars (literally $3). You can usually find better discounts on hotel booking sites or even booking directly with the hotel of your choice. Instead of offering a minimal discount at one hotel, we figured our guests would be better off choosing what works best for them. Here are a few suggestions in a couple of price points.
If you aren’t too familiar with Indianapolis, we recommend staying on the north side of town, it’s not too far from the venue and located off major highways for easy access.

All these hotels and the others off the Meridian and Keystone exits of I-465 take roughly 15 minutes from the hotel to our wedding venue.
  • Holiday Inn Indianapolis Carmel
  • Drury Plaza Hotel Indianapolis Carmel
  • Indianapolis Marriott North
If you want to be even closer to the venue, or want a homey stay there are plenty of Airbnb’s in Indy to choose from at a variety of price points as well. There are even a handful within walking distance from the Art Center!

Things to Do in Indy

We love Indianapolis and if you are looking for some things to do over the weekend (other than watch us get married), here are a few possible options.
For the Kiddos!
We recommend: The Indianapolis Children’s Museum is the biggest children’s museum in the world! It’s a huge space with everything from dinosaurs, hands on exhibits for all ages, and a new outdoor sports area!
Other places of note: the Indianapolis Zoo and the White River Canal.

Sports!
We recommend: The Indianapolis Motor Speedway. There are tours that drive around the track (in a bus) and some let you get out at the line of bricks.
Other places of note: the NCAA Hall of Fame is located off the White River Canal, there is also a Colts game on Sunday the 22nd if that’s your jam! P.S. They’re playing the Falcons.
Gettin Artsy!
We recommend: Newfields (formerly the Indianapolis Art Museum)- the modern art is some of our favorites, and the grounds are a beautiful place to explore (or enjoy the beer garden).
Other places of note: the Eiteljorg Museum for Native American and Western Art (bonus- explore murals and outdoor sculpture right outside on the White River Canal!)

Foodies!
We LOVE the restaurants in Indy and it’s hard to go wrong with so many good options.
We recommend: the Eagle (amazing fried chicken), Mimi Blue’s Meatballs (delicious meatballs, we were skeptical at first but it’s great!- great vegan/vegetarian options too), Bluebeard or Hedge Row for a hip, trendy, night out, any of the local breweries or distilleries.
